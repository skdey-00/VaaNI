# Complaint Lodging and Resolution Process

This document outlines the comprehensive process for handling customer complaints in a bank branch.

## RBI Regulations on Complaint Management

As per RBI Consumer Education and Protection Cell, banks must have:
1. **Complaint Redressal Mechanism**: Proper system to receive and resolve complaints
2. **Time-bound Resolution**: Specific turnaround times for different complaint types
3. **Complaint Acknowledgement**: Unique reference number for each complaint
4. **Grievance Redressal Officer**: Designated officer for complaints
5. **Reporting**: Quarterly reporting of complaints to RBI

## Complaint Lodging Process

### Step 1: Complaint Receipt and Registration

**Customer Reception:**
- Greet customer warmly and calmly
- Listen patiently to the customer's grievance
- Do not interrupt or argue
- Show empathy and understanding
- Maintain eye contact and professional demeanor
- Note down key points of the complaint

**Initial Assessment:**
- Understand the nature of the complaint
- Identify the category (transaction, service, account, etc.)
- Assess severity (urgent / routine)
- Determine if immediate resolution is possible

**Acknowledge and Empathize:**
- "I understand your concern, Mr./Ms. [Name]"
- "I apologize for the inconvenience caused"
- "Let me look into this matter immediately"
- "I will help you resolve this issue"

**Important:**
- Do not blame the customer
- Do not blame other colleagues in front of customer
- Do not make excuses
- Focus on solution, not problem

### Step 2: Complaint Recording

**Complaint Register Entry:**
Every complaint must be recorded in the Complaint Register with following details:

1. **Complaint Number**: Unique sequential number
2. **Date**: Date of complaint receipt
3. **Time**: Time of complaint receipt
4. **Customer Name**: Full name of complainant
5. **Account Number**: Customer's account number (if applicable)
6. **Contact Number**: Customer's mobile number
7. **Email Address**: Customer's email ID (if available)
8. **Complaint Category**: Account/Transaction/Service/etc.
9. **Complaint Details**: Detailed description of the grievance
10. **Documents Available**: Relevant documents submitted
11. **Priority**: High / Medium / Low
12. **Assigned To**: Name of officer handling the complaint
13. **Status**: Pending / Under Investigation / Resolved / Closed
14. **Resolution Date**: Date when resolved
15. **Resolution Details**: How the complaint was resolved
16. **Customer Satisfaction**: Satisfied / Not Satisfied

**Complaint Ticket in System:**
In addition to physical register, enter complaint in Core Banking System or Complaint Management System (CMS):
- Generate unique complaint ticket number
- Format: CPGYYYYMMDDXXXX (CPG = Complaint, date, sequence)
- Example: CPG20231215001 (Complaint #001 on 15 Dec 2023)

**Priority Assessment:**

| Priority | Response Time | Description |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| **High / Urgent** | Immediate | Financial loss, unauthorized transaction, elderly/disabled customer, media attention |
| **Medium** | Within 24 hours | Service issues, delay, wrong charges |
| **Low** | Within 3-5 days | General grievances, suggestions |

### Step 3: Acknowledgement to Customer

**Provide Acknowledgement:**
Provide written acknowledgement containing:
1. **Complaint Reference Number**: Unique ticket number
2. **Date and Time**: Of complaint receipt
3. **Complaint Summary**: Brief description
4. **Expected Resolution Time**: TAT as per category
5. **Contact Person**: Name and contact of handling officer
6. **Customer Care Number**: 24x7 toll-free number for follow-up

**Acknowledgement Formats:**
- **Physical**: Acknowledgement slip/copy of complaint form
- **SMS**: SMS acknowledgement to registered mobile
- **Email**: Email acknowledgement with complaint reference
- **WhatsApp**: For banks with WhatsApp banking (optional)

**Sample Acknowledgement SMS:**
```
Thank you for contacting [Bank Name]. Your complaint has been registered.
Complaint No: CPG20231215001
Expected Resolution: [Date]
For queries: 1800-XXX-XXXX
```

### Step 4: Complaint Investigation

**Preliminary Check:**
1. Verify customer details
2. Check account/transaction history
3. Identify root cause of the issue
4. Determine if it's a genuine complaint

**Investigation Process:**

**For Transaction Failures:**
- Check if debit happened
- Check if credit happened at beneficiary end
- Check if reversal initiated
- Check timeout/technical failure logs
- Verify with IT department if needed

**For ATM Issues:**
- Verify from ATM switch
- Check settlement files
- Verify CCTV footage (for disputes)
- Contact ATM managing department

**For Service Issues:**
- Talk to staff involved
- Check branch records
- Verify if procedures followed
- Identify process gaps

**For Charges-Related:**
- Verify charges applied
- Check if charges are as per schedule
- Verify if waiver is applicable
- Check customer's account type/segment

**Gather Evidence:**
- Screenshots
- Transaction logs
- System records
- Statements
- Correspondence
- CCTV footage (if applicable)

### Step 5: Resolution Processing

**Immediate Resolution (If Possible):**
For complaints that can be resolved immediately:
- Process refund/credit immediately
- Reverse charges/penalties
- Correct account errors
- Issue requested documents
- Unlock account/card
- Reset password/PIN
- Close account (if requested)

**Examples of Immediate Resolution:**
- Wrong charge reversal
- Duplicate debit reversal
- Account unfreezing (after KYC)
- PIN reset
- Password reset
- Statement request
- Cheque book request

**Escalation (If Cannot Resolve at Branch):**
If resolution requires departmental action:
1. **Credit Card**: Escalate to credit card department
2. ATM Issues**: Escalate to ATM switch team
3. Loans**: Escalate to loans department
4. Digital Banking**: Escalate to IT/Digital Banking team
5. Technical Issues**: Escalate to IT Support

**Escalation Process:**
- Fill escalation form with complaint details
- Attach all relevant documents
- Send to concerned department
- Obtain acknowledgement from department
- Note escalation date
- Follow up after specified TAT

### Step 6: Communication with Customer

**Regular Updates:**
Keep customer informed at every stage:
- When complaint is registered (initial acknowledgement)
- When investigation is in progress
- When resolution is initiated
- When complaint is resolved/closed

**Update Frequency:**
- **High Priority**: Daily update
- **Medium Priority**: Update every 2-3 days
- **Low Priority**: Weekly update

**Communication Channels:**
- Phone call
- SMS
- Email
- Letter (for formal resolutions)

**Sample Update Communication:**
```
Dear Mr./Ms. [Name],

Regarding your complaint [Complaint Number], we wish to inform you that we are
investigating the matter. We expect to resolve it by [Date]. We appreciate your
patience.

For queries: Contact [Officer Name] at [Phone] / [Email]
```

### Step 7: Resolution and Closure

**Resolution Actions:**
Based on investigation, take appropriate action:

1. **Credit Customer Account**: For wrongful debit, refund with interest (if applicable)
2. **Waive Charges**: Reverse wrong charges/penalties
3. **Correction**: Update records, correct errors
4. **Service Delivery**: Provide the service requested
5. **Apology**: Offer sincere apology for inconvenience
6. **Compensation**: For financial loss (assessed case-by-case)
7. **Process Improvement**: Identify root cause and prevent recurrence

**For Failed ATM Transactions (Cash Not Dispensed):**
- Verify from ATM logs
- Check if amount debited
- Process refund within 7 working days (RBI mandate)
- Inform customer once refund processed
- If not resolved in 7 days: Pay ₹100 per day of delay

**For Unauthorized Transactions:**
- Block card/account immediately
- Lodge complaint with cyber cell (if fraud)
- Reverse unauthorized transaction (after investigation)
- Issue new card
- Educate customer on security

**Resolution Timeline (RBI Guidelines):**

| Complaint Type | Resolution Time |
|----------------|-----------------|
| ATM Cash Not Dispensed | 7 days |
| Failed Electronic Transaction | 7-10 days |
| Wrong Debit/Charge | 7-10 days |
| Account Not Opened | 15-30 days |
| Loan/Card Issues | 30 days |
| General Service Issues | 30 days |
| Complex Cases | 60 days |

**If Resolution Delayed:**
- Pay compensation to customer (₹100 per day for ATM failures, beyond 7 days)
- Inform customer about delay and reason
- Provide revised resolution timeline
- Escalate to higher authority

### Step 8: Customer Confirmation and Feedback

**Obtain Customer Confirmation:**
Once resolution is processed:
- Contact customer and inform about resolution
- Get customer's confirmation that issue is resolved
- Ask for feedback/satisfaction

**Feedback Collection:**
- Ask customer: "Are you satisfied with the resolution?"
- Record feedback in complaint register
- If not satisfied: Understand reasons, take additional action

**Customer Feedback Questions:**
1. Was your complaint resolved?
2. Were you satisfied with the resolution?
3. Was the resolution timely?
4. Any suggestions for improvement?

**Close Complaint:**
- Update complaint status to "Closed - Resolved"
- Update closure date
- Record resolution details
- Obtain customer signature on closure document

**If Customer Not Satisfied:**
- Escalate to Branch Manager
- Offer alternative resolution
- If still not satisfied: Explain escalation options
- Provide details of Banking Ombudsman
- Help customer approach higher authority

## Escalation Matrix

### Level 1: Branch Level
- **Contact**: Branch Operations Manager / Branch Manager
- **Timeline**: Resolution within 7-30 days based on complaint type

### Level 2: Regional Office / Zonal Office
- **When**: If not resolved at branch within stipulated time
- **Contact**: Regional Manager / Zonal Manager
- **Timeline**: 15-30 days for resolution

### Level 3: Customer Service Department (Head Office)
- **When**: If not resolved at regional level
- **Contact**: Customer Service / Nodal Officer
- **Timeline**: 30 days for resolution

### Level 4: Banking Ombudsman (RBI)
- **When**: If bank does not respond within 30 days OR customer not satisfied with resolution
- **Where**: File complaint online at https://cms.rbi.org.in or write to ombudsman
- **Timeline**: Ombudsman resolves within 30-60 days
- **Attachment**: Complaint copy, bank's rejection letter (if any)

**Note**: Customer can approach Banking Ombudsman only after exhausting bank's internal grievance redressal mechanism.

## Special Category Complaints

### Elderly / Differently-abled Customers
- **Priority**: High priority
- **Handling**: Special assistance, patient handling
- **Response**: Immediate response
- **Resolution**: Priority resolution

### Illiterate Customers
- **Patience**: Extra patience and explanation
- **Assistance**: Help fill complaint form
- **Thumb Impression**: Allow LTI
- **Follow-up**: Proactive follow-up and updates

### Non-Resident Indians (NRIs)
- **Channel**: Email / Online complaint preferred
- **Resolution Time**: May take longer due to distance
- **Communication**: Email communication
- **Time Zone**: Consider time zone differences

### Fraud-Related Complaints
- **Action**: Immediate report to cyber cell / police
- **Blocking**: Immediate block of card/account
- **Zero Liability**: If reported within 3 days (for card fraud)
- **Investigation**: Detailed investigation required
- **Legal**: May involve legal proceedings

## Complaint Prevention

### Process Improvement
Based on complaint patterns:
- Identify root causes
- Improve processes
- Train staff
- Update systems
- Implement preventive controls

### Common Complaint Areas and Prevention

| Complaint Type | Prevention Measure |
|----------------|-------------------|
| ATM cash not dispensed | Improve ATM maintenance, reduce downtime |
| Failed transactions | Improve IT systems, reduce technical glitches |
| Staff behavior | Regular soft skills training, customer service training |
| Long waiting time | More counters, better queue management |
| Wrong charges | Automated charge calculation, proper verification |
| Non-delivery of statements | Promote e-statements, verify postal address |

## Common Scenarios and Handling

### Scenario 1: Angry customer shouting at branch

**Action:**
1. Stay calm
2. Don't argue
3. Invite to cabin (away from public area)
4. Listen patiently
5. Acknowledge concern
6. Apologize for inconvenience
7. Offer water (helps calm customer)
8. Explain what will be done
9. Set realistic expectations
10. Provide complaint reference

### Scenario 2: Customer says "I will close my account"

**Action:**
1. Acknowledge customer's concern
2. Apologize for the issue
3. Assure resolution
4. Explain that closing account is customer's choice
5. Request opportunity to resolve first
6. If still wants to close: Accept gracefully, help with closure
7. Don't force customer to stay

### Scenario 3: Customer threatens to approach media/regulator

**Action:**
1. Don't panic
2. Don't be defensive
3. Acknowledge customer's right
4. Assure best effort to resolve
5. Provide prompt resolution
6. Document conversation
7. Inform Branch Manager
8. Escalate for priority resolution

### Scenario 4: Multiple complaints on same issue

**Action:**
1. Identify pattern
2. Root cause analysis
3. Process/system correction
4. Preventive measures
5. Report to higher management for systemic resolution

## Customer Rights (RBI Charter)

Every customer has right to:
1. **Fair Treatment**: Transparent and fair treatment
2. **Timely Resolution**: Time-bound resolution of complaints
3. **Information**: Clear information about products/services
4. **Privacy**: Confidentiality of customer information
5. **Grievance Redressal**: Access to grievance redressal mechanism
6. **Compensation**: For deficiencies in service (as per RBI guidelines)

## Branch Staff Responsibilities

### Do's
- Listen patiently and empathetically
- Acknowledge complaint with apology
- Provide unique complaint reference
- Set realistic resolution time
- Keep customer informed
- Resolve within TAT
- Document everything
- Learn from complaints and improve

### Don'ts
- Don't argue with customer
- Don't blame customer
- Don't blame colleagues
- Don't make false promises
- Don't ignore complaint
- Don't delay acknowledgement
- Don't share customer's complaint details publicly
- Don't lose temper
