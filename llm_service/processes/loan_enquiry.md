# Loan Enquiry and Application Process

This document outlines the comprehensive process for handling loan enquiries and applications in a bank branch.

## Types of Loans Offered

### Secured Loans
1. **Home Loan** - For purchase/construction/renovation of residential property
2. **Loan Against Property (LAP)** - Secured loan against residential/commercial property
3. **Auto Loan** - For purchase of new/used cars and two-wheelers
4. **Gold Loan** - Secured against gold jewelry/coins

### Unsecured Loans
1. **Personal Loan** - For various personal needs (medical, travel, wedding)
2. **Business Loan** - For business expansion, working capital
3. **Education Loan** - For higher studies in India/abroad
4. **Professional Loan** - For doctors, lawyers, chartered accountants

## Loan Enquiry Handling Process

### Step 1: Customer Greeting and Need Assessment

**Reception:**
- Welcome customer warmly
- Ask for the purpose of loan
- Understand customer's profile:
  - Employment type (salaried/self-employed/professional)
  - Income level
  - Loan amount required
  - Preferred tenure

**Information Gathering:**
- Why do you need the loan?
- How much amount do you need?
- What is your preferred repayment period?
- Do you have any existing loans?
- Do you have any assets to pledge?

### Step 2: Loan Product Explanation

Based on customer's requirement, explain suitable loan options:

**Home Loan:**
- Loan amount: Up to ₹5 crore (varies by bank)
- Tenure: Up to 30 years
- Interest rate: 8.35% - 10.5% (floating) (varies by market)
- Processing fee: 0.5% - 1% of loan amount
- Tax benefits under Section 80C and 24(b)
- Required: Property documents, income proof

**Personal Loan:**
- Loan amount: ₹50,000 - ₹40 lakh
- Tenure: 1-5 years
- Interest rate: 10.5% - 18% (fixed)
- Processing fee: 1% - 2.5% of loan amount
- No collateral required
- Required: Income proof, employment stability

**Auto Loan (New Car):**
- Loan amount: Up to 90% of on-road price
- Tenure: 1-7 years
- Interest rate: 7.5% - 11%
- Processing fee: 0.5% - 1%
- Required: Income proof, vehicle quotation, proforma invoice

**Gold Loan:**
- Loan amount: Up to 75% of gold value
- Tenure: 3-12 months (can be renewed)
- Interest rate: 7% - 12%
- Processing fee: Nil or minimal
- Required: Gold jewelry, identity proof, address proof
- Quick disbursal: Same day

### Step 3: Eligibility Assessment

**For Salaried Individuals:**

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- Minimum age: 21 years at application
- Maximum age at maturity: 58-60 years (salaried)
- Minimum net monthly income: ₹20,000 - ₹30,000
- Minimum work experience: 2 years in current job, 3 years total
- Minimum CIBIL score: 700 (preferred 750+)

**Income Calculation:**
- Take-home salary should be minimum 40% of gross after loan EMI
- For existing EMI: Add to new EMI for calculation
- FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio): Should be ≤ 50-60%

**Loan Amount Calculation:**
```
Monthly EMI = [P x R x (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N-1]
where P = Loan amount, R = Monthly interest rate, N = Tenure in months

Eligible Loan Amount = (Net Monthly Income x FOIR%) - Existing EMI / EMI factor
```

**Example:**
- Monthly income: ₹50,000
- FOIR: 50%
- Existing EMI: ₹5,000
- EMI capacity: ₹50,000 x 50% - ₹5,000 = ₹20,000
- At 10% interest for 5 years: Eligible loan ~₹9.4 lakh

**For Self-Employed Professionals:**

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- Minimum age: 23-25 years
- Maximum age: 65 years at maturity
- Business vintage: Minimum 3-5 years
- Minimum annual income: ₹4-5 lakh
- Profit shown in ITR: Required
- Minimum CIBIL score: 700

**Documents Required:**
- Business registration certificate
- Last 3 years ITR with computation of income
- Last 6 months/1 year bank statements
- GST returns (if registered)
- Business proof: Shop & Establishment certificate, trade license
- Office address proof: Utility bill, rent agreement

**For Self-Employed Non-Professionals (Businessmen):**

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- Business vintage: Minimum 5 years (stable)
- Business should be profit-making
- Minimum annual turnover: ₹20-30 lakh
- Minimum CIBIL score: 700

**Additional Requirements:**
- Last 3 years audited financial statements
- Business continuity proof
- Profit & Loss account, Balance Sheet
- Partnership deed (if partnership firm)
- MOA & AOA (if company)

### Step 4: Credit Score (CIBIL) Check

**Process:**
1. Obtain customer consent for CIBIL check
2. Access CIBIL report via authorized bureau
3. Review the following:
   - CIBIL Score (300-900):
     - 750+: Excellent
     - 700-749: Good
     - 650-699: Fair
     - Below 650: Poor (high rejection risk)
   - Payment history: Any missed/late payments
   - Credit utilization: Should be below 30-40%
   - Number of inquiries: Too many inquiries indicate credit hunger
   - Outstanding loans and EMIs
   - Settlements/written-off accounts

**Actions based on CIBIL:**
- **Score 750+**: Proceed with application
- **Score 700-749**: Can consider with strong income profile
- **Score 650-699**: Higher interest rate, lower loan amount, or guarantor
- **Score below 650**: Generally decline, unless explained
- **Default/Settlement**: Need explanation, may require guarantor or collateral

### Step 5: EMI Calculation and Repayment Schedule

**EMI Computation:**
- Use loan EMI calculator
- Provide amortization schedule
- Show principal and interest breakup
- Explain prepayment options and charges

**Prepayment Illustration:**
- Show interest savings on prepayment
- Explain prepayment charges (usually 2-5% of outstanding principal)
- Explain part prepayment facility

**Example for ₹10 lakh loan at 10% for 5 years:**
```
Loan Amount: ₹10,00,000
Interest Rate: 10% p.a.
Tenure: 5 years (60 months)
Monthly EMI: ₹21,247
Total Interest: ₹2,74,820
Total Amount: ₹12,74,820
```

### Step 6: Document Collection

**For Salaried Individuals:**

**Identity Proof:**
- PAN Card (mandatory)
- Aadhaar Card
- Passport/Voter ID/Driving License (any one)

**Address Proof:**
- Aadhaar Card (current address)
- Passport
- Utility bill (electricity, telephone) - latest
- Bank passbook with address

**Income Proof:**
- Last 3 months salary slips (with all deductions shown)
- Last 2 years Form 16 / Form 26AS
- Last 6 months salary credit bank statement (with salary credited)
- Employment certificate (mentioning tenure, designation, salary)
- Appointment letter / ID card copy

**For Self-Employed:**

**Identity & Address Proof:** Same as above

**Business Proof:**
- Business registration certificate
- Shop & Establishment registration
- GST registration (if applicable)
- Trade license

**Income Proof:**
- Last 3 years Income Tax Returns (ITR)
- Last 3 years computation of income
- P&L account and Balance Sheet for last 3 years
- Last 1 year business/current account bank statement
- GST returns and audit report (if applicable)
- Professional degree certificate (for professionals)

**For Home Loan (Additional):**
- Property papers: Sale agreement/construction agreement
- Property title documents
- Chain of previous agreements (if resale)
- Approved building plan
- NOC from society/builder
- Property tax receipts
- Encumbrance certificate (EC)

**For Auto Loan (Additional):**
- Proforma invoice of vehicle
- Dealer quotation
- Vehicle registration quotation
- Existing vehicle RC (if exchange)

**For Gold Loan:**
- Gold jewelry for valuation
- Invoice of jewelry (if available)
- Security agreement form

### Step 7: Loan Application Processing

**Application Form Filling:**
- Fill loan application form with complete details
- Get customer signature on all required places
- Collect photograph
- Obtain PAN verification online

**Documentation:**
- Verify all original documents
- Take self-attested copies
- Complete document checklist
- Sign and stamp as verifier

**Application Stage:**
1. Enter application in LOS (Loan Origination System)
2. Assign application number
3. Attach scanned documents
4. Conduct credit verification:
   - Call employer to verify employment
   - Verify residence (if needed)
   - Verify bank statements
5. Get field investigation (FI) report for high-value loans

### Step 8: Credit Appraisal

**Credit Analysis:**

**Income Analysis:**
- Verify income documents
- Check consistency across documents
- Assess income stability and growth
- Calculate net monthly income

**Repayment Capacity:**
- Calculate FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio)
- Calculate EMI to income ratio
- Assess disposable income after EMI

**Collateral Assessment** (for secured loans):
- Property valuation by approved valuer
- Legal verification of property documents
- Title search
- Encumbrance check

**Guarantor Assessment** (if applicable):
- Guarantor's KYC
- Guarantor's income proof
- Guarantor's credit score
- Guarantee agreement execution

**Risk Grading:**
Based on profile, assign risk grade:
- **Low Risk**: High income, stable job, high CIBIL, existing relationship
- **Medium Risk**: Adequate income, moderate CIBIL, some existing loans
- **High Risk**: Low income, low CIBIL, unstable employment

**Approval Decision:**
- Approve with standard terms
- Approve with modified terms (lower amount, higher rate, shorter tenure)
- Decline with reason

### Step 9: Sanction and Offer Letter

**If Approved:**
1. Generate sanction letter with:
   - Sanctioned loan amount
   - Applicable interest rate
   - Tenure
   - EMI amount
   - Processing fee details
   - Terms and conditions
   - Documents required for disbursement

2. Get customer acceptance on sanction letter
3. Customer signs acceptance copy

**If Declined:**
1. Generate decline letter with reason
2. Communicate to customer
3. Retain application record for specified period

**Common Decline Reasons:**
- Low CIBIL score
- Insufficient income
- High existing obligations
- Unstable employment
- Negative credit history (defaults, settlements)
- Incomplete documents

### Step 10: Security Creation and Disbursement

**Pre-Disbursement:**
- Create security charge on collateral (for secured loans)
- Get loan agreement signed
- Get post-dated cheques (PDCs) or ECS mandate
- Get standing instruction for auto-debit
- Verify insurance of asset (for auto/home loan)
- Link loan account with customer account

**Disbursement:**
- **For Home Loan:**
  - Disburse directly to builder/seller
  - In tranches based on construction stage
  - Obtain demand from builder
  - Verify stage completion before disbursement

- **For Auto Loan:**
  - Disburse directly to dealer
  - Get proforma invoice and dealer confirmation
  - Ensure vehicle registration in bank's name

- **For Personal Loan:**
  - Credit to customer's savings account
  - After deduction of processing fee
  - Direct credit or via cheque

- **For Gold Loan:**
  - Credit to customer account after gold valuation
  - Secure gold in bank locker
  - Provide gold receipt

## Post-Disbursement Process

### Customer Communication
- Send welcome kit with:
  - Loan account number
  - EMI schedule
  - Payment instructions
  - Contact details for loan service
  - Insurance policy (if applicable)

### EMI Payment Options
- **NACH (National Automated Clearing House):** Auto-debit from account
- **ECS Mandate:** Auto-debit instructions
- **Post-Dated Cheques (PDCs):** 36-60 PDCs
- **Standing Instruction:** Auto-debit from account
- **Online Payment:** Net banking, mobile banking, UPI

### EMI Reminder
- Send SMS reminder 3 days before due date
- Email reminder before due date
- Confirm EMI debit after payment

### Prepayment Handling
**Part Prepayment:**
- Accept minimum amount (usually ₹10,000 or one EMI, whichever is higher)
- Calculate reduction in tenure or EMI
- Update amortization schedule
- Issue acknowledgment

**Full Prepayment (Foreclosure):**
- Calculate outstanding principal
- Add prepayment charges (usually 2-5%)
- Issue No Dues Certificate (NDC)
- Close loan account
- Release collateral security
- Update CIBIL (report as closed)

## NPA (Non-Performing Asset) Management

**Classification:**
- **NPA:** If EMI overdue for 90 days+
- **Sub-standard:** NPA for up to 12 months
- **Doubtful:** NPA for 12+ months
- **Loss:** Uncertain recovery

**Recovery Process:**
1. Day 1-30: Reminder calls and messages
2. Day 31-60: Notice to primary and guarantor
3. Day 61-90: Legal notice
4. Day 91+: NPA classification, recovery proceedings
5. SARFAESI Act action (for secured loans)
6. Asset possession and auction

## Turnaround Time (TAT)

| Stage | Time Period |
|-------|-------------|
| Application to Sanction | 3-7 working days |
| Disbursement after acceptance | 2-5 working days |
| Home Loan Disbursement | 7-15 working days (due to property verification) |
| Personal Loan Disbursement | 24-48 hours |
| Gold Loan Disbursement | Same day (within hours) |
| Auto Loan Disbursement | 2-3 working days |

## Important Points for Branch Staff

1. **Never promise guaranteed approval**: Always mention "subject to approval"
2. **Be transparent about charges**: Explain processing fee, prepayment charges
3. **Don't mislead about interest rates**: Explain flat vs reducing balance
4. **Explain the impact of EMI on income**: Show disposable income calculation
5. **Don't force customers to take loans**: Customer should make informed decision
6. **Never accept cash for processing fee**: Only accept cheque/draft/account debit
7. **Don't share customer's CIBIL details**: Confidential information
8. **Always get customer consent before CIBIL check**: Legal requirement
9. **Explain insurance requirement**: For home/auto loans, insurance is mandatory
10. **Guide on loan protection insurance**: Explain benefits of loan insurance
