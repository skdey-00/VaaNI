# Fixed Deposit Booking Process

This document outlines the process for opening and managing Fixed Deposit (FD) accounts, also known as Term Deposits.

## Types of Fixed Deposits

### 1. Regular Fixed Deposit
- Lump sum deposit for fixed tenure
- Interest paid monthly/quarterly or at maturity
- Premature withdrawal allowed with penalty
- Minimum amount: ₹10,000
- Tenure options: 7 days to 10 years

### 2. Tax Saving Fixed Deposit (5-Year FD)
- Lock-in period of 5 years
- No premature withdrawal allowed
- Tax deduction under Section 80C (up to ₹1.5 lakh)
- Interest taxable
- Minimum amount: ₹100 (usually ₹10,000)
- Maximum amount: ₹1.5 lakh for tax benefit

### 3. Special FD Schemes
- **Senior Citizen FD**: Higher interest rate (usually 0.25% - 0.50% extra)
- **NRE FD**: For NRIs, tax-free interest in India
- **NRO FD**: For NRIs, interest taxable
- **Staff FD**: For bank employees, special rates

### 4. Cumulative vs Non-Cumulative FD
- **Cumulative**: Interest reinvested, paid at maturity (higher effective yield)
- **Non-Cumulative**: Interest paid periodically (monthly/quarterly)

### 5. Flexi FD / Sweep-in FD
- Linked to savings account
- Auto-sweep excess balance to FD
- Auto-credit from FD when balance low
- Better interest on surplus funds

## FD Booking Procedure

### Step 1: Customer Enquiry and Requirement Assessment

**Customer Greeting:**
- Welcome customer
- Understand FD requirements:
  - Amount to deposit
  - Preferred tenure
  - Interest payout preference (monthly/quarterly/at maturity)
  - Type of FD (regular, tax-saving, NRE/NRO)

**Information Provided:**
1. Current FD interest rates for various tenures
2. Maturity amount calculation
3. Premature withdrawal rules
4. TDS deduction on interest
5. Loan against FD facility
6. Auto-renewal options

### Step 2: Customer Verification and KYC

**For Existing Customers:**
- Verify customer details in system
- Ensure KYC is current
- Update if any change in PAN, address, nomination

**For New Customers:**
- Complete full KYC procedure (refer KYC Verification Process)
- Obtain:
  - PAN Card (mandatory for TDS)
  - Aadhaar Card / Passport / Voter ID
  - Passport-size photograph
  - PAN verification (if PAN not available in system)

### Step 3: FD Amount and Tenure Selection

**FD Amount:**
- Minimum amount: Usually ₹10,000 (varies by bank)
- Maximum amount: No upper limit
- For cumulative interest, minimum may be higher

**Tenure Selection Guide:**
- **7-90 days**: For parking short-term surplus funds
- **91-180 days**: Medium-term, better interest
- **1-2 years**: Optimal interest rates
- **3-5 years**: Higher rates for long-term commitment
- **5 years**: Tax saving FD (lock-in, tax benefit)

**Recommendation Strategy:**
- **FD Laddering**: Suggest multiple FDs with different tenures
  - Example: Divide ₹5 lakh into 5 FDs of ₹1 lakh each
  - Tenures: 1 year, 2 years, 3 years, 4 years, 5 years
  - Benefits: Regular liquidity, better average interest, reinvestment at maturity

**Maturity Calculation:**
```
Maturity Amount = Principal × (1 + Rate/N)^(N×T)
Where:
- Principal = Deposit amount
- Rate = Annual interest rate
- N = Compounding frequency (4 for quarterly, 2 for half-yearly)
- T = Tenure in years
```

**Example: ₹1,00,000 for 2 years at 7% p.a. compounded quarterly:**
```
Quarterly rate = 7%/4 = 1.75% = 0.0175
Periods = 2 × 4 = 8 quarters
Maturity Amount = 1,00,000 × (1.0175)^8
Maturity Amount = 1,00,000 × 1.149 = ₹1,14,900
```

### Step 4: Nomination Details

**RBI Requirement:**
- Nomination is **mandatory** for all FD accounts
- Customer can nominate one or more nominees
- Nominee can be changed during FD tenure

**Nomination Form Details:**
- Nominee's full name
- Relationship with customer
- Nominee's address
- Nominee's date of birth (if minor)
- Guardian details (if nominee is minor)
- Share of each nominee (if multiple nominees)

**If Customer Refuses Nomination:**
- Explain nomination importance
- Get signature in "No Nomination" column
- Obtain written declaration for refusal

### Step 5: Interest Payout Instructions

**Payout Options:**
1. **Monthly Credit**: Interest credited to savings account monthly
2. **Quarterly Credit**: Interest credited quarterly
3. **Half-Yearly Credit**: Interest credited every 6 months
4. **At Maturity**: Interest paid along with principal at maturity
5. **Reinvestment (Cumulative)**: Interest reinvested quarterly

**Payout Account:**
- Savings or Current account with the same bank
- Interest credit to same branch or other branch
- If no account, open savings account first

**Auto-Renewal Option:**
- **With Principal**: Both principal + interest reinvested
- **With Principal + Interest**: Only principal reinvested, interest paid out
- **One-time renewal**: Renew only once, then credit to account
- **No Renewal**: Credit maturity proceeds to account

### Step 6: Form Filling and Documentation

**FD Opening Form Details:**
- Customer name and account number
- FD deposit amount (in words and figures)
- FD tenure (in days/months/years)
- Maturity date calculation
- Interest rate applied
- Maturity amount calculation
- Payout instructions
- Nomination details
- Auto-renewal instructions
- Customer signature/thumb impression

**Documents Attached:**
- PAN card copy (mandatory for TDS)
- Customer photograph
- KYC documents (for new customers)
- Deposit slip for debit amount

### Step 7: Fund Debit

**Payment Options:**
1. **Cash**: Accept cash up to ₹50,000 (PAN required for ₹50,000+)
2. **Cheque**: Local/Outstation cheque (clearing period applies)
3. **Account Debit**: Debit customer's savings/current account
4. **Transfer**: NEFT/RTGS from other bank

**Cash Deposit Rule:**
- PAN required for cash deposit of ₹50,000+ in single FD
- PAN required for aggregate cash deposits of ₹5 lakh+ in a year
- Form 60/61 if PAN not available

**Cheque Deposit:**
- Note cheque number, date, bank, and amount
- Write "FD deposit for [customer name]" on cheque
- FD value date will be after clearing (usually 2-3 working days)
- Interest accrual starts from value date

**Account Debit:**
- Debit customer's account immediately
- FD value date is same day
- Get customer's signature on debit authorization

### Step 8: FD Certificate Issuance

**FD Receipt/Certificate Contents:**
- FD account number
- Customer name and address
- Deposit amount
- Date of deposit
- Maturity date
- Interest rate (annual)
- Maturity amount
- Payout instructions
- Renewal instructions
- Nomination details
- Branch details and contact
- Authorised signatory signature and stamp

**Delivery Options:**
- Physical FD receipt handed to customer
- Email soft copy to customer
- Both physical and soft copy

**Customer Confirmation:**
- Customer acknowledges receipt of FD certificate
- Check all details are correct
- Sign in FD register

### Step 9: System Entry and Record Keeping

**Update Core Banking System:**
- Create FD account in system
- Link to customer's CIF
- Enter all parameters
- Upload scanned documents

**Record Keeping:**
- Enter in FD register
- File documents physically
- Update customer profile

## FD Interest and Taxation

### TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)

**TDS Threshold:**
- **For individuals below 60 years**: TDS if interest > ₹40,000 per year per bank
- **For senior citizens (60+ years)**: TDS if interest > ₹50,000 per year per bank
- **TDS Rate**: 10% if PAN provided, 20% if PAN not provided

**TDS Calculation Example:**
- FD of ₹5,00,000 at 7% for 1 year
- Interest = ₹35,000
- Below threshold, no TDS

- FD of ₹5,00,000 at 7% for 2 years
- Year 1 interest = ₹35,000 (no TDS)
- Year 2 interest = ₹37,450 (cumulative) (no TDS)
- Total interest ₹72,450 (no TDS, because calculated annually)

**Form 15G/15H (No TDS Declaration):**
- **Form 15G**: For individuals below 60 years
- **Form 15H**: For senior citizens (60+ years)
- Submit if total taxable income is below threshold
- Submit at beginning of financial year
- Valid for one financial year

### Interest Payment Schedule

**Monthly Payout:**
- Interest calculated monthly
- Credited to savings account
- Monthly interest = Principal × Annual Rate / 12

**Quarterly Payout:**
- Interest calculated quarterly
- Credited in March, June, September, December
- Quarterly interest = Principal × Annual Rate / 4

**Cumulative FD:**
- Interest compounded quarterly
- Reinvested in FD
- Higher effective yield

## Premature Withdrawal of FD

### Rules and Procedure

**Penalty for Premature Withdrawal:**
- Usually 0.50% - 1.00% penalty on applicable rate
- Applicable rate is the rate for the period FD actually remained

**Example:**
- FD booked for 2 years at 7.5%
- Prematurely closed after 6 months
- Applicable rate for 6 months = 4.5%
- Penalty = 1%
- Actual rate applied = 4.5% - 1% = 3.5%

**Procedure:**
1. Customer submits premature withdrawal request
2. Calculate premature amount:
   - Principal + Interest at revised rate (with penalty)
   - TDS deduction on interest earned
3. Debit FD account
4. Credit customer's account or pay by cheque
5. Close FD account
6. Issue closure receipt

**Exceptions to Penalty:**
- Death of depositor: No penalty (credit nominee's account)
- Staff FD: No penalty for bank employees
- Compromise cases: Manager's discretion (rare)

## Loan Against FD

### Features
- Loan amount: Up to 90-95% of FD amount
- Interest rate: FD rate + 1-2%
- Processing time: Same day
- No prepayment charges
- No foreclosure charges

### Procedure
1. Customer requests loan against FD
2. Obtain request letter mentioning loan amount
3. Create lien on FD
4. Sanction loan (usually same day)
5. Disburse to customer's account
6. FD continues to earn interest

### Repayment
- EMI or bullet repayment
- FD lien released after full repayment
- If default, FD adjusted against loan

## FD Maturity and Renewal

### At Maturity

**Auto-Renewal Enabled:**
- System automatically renews FD
- Send renewal confirmation to customer
- Issue new FD certificate

**No Auto-Renewal:**
- Credit maturity amount to customer's account
- Send maturity intimation
- Close FD account

### Partial Withdrawal (Not Common)
- Most banks don't allow partial withdrawal
- Some allow multiple FDs, close one if needed
- Better: Use FD laddering for liquidity

## FD Operations and Services

### Change of Tenure
- Not allowed before maturity
- Premature closure required
- Book new FD with desired tenure

### Change of Payout Instructions
- Submit request letter
- Change from monthly to quarterly payout
- Change from cumulative to non-cumulative (premature + new FD)

### Nomination Change
- Submit new nomination form
- Previous nomination revoked
- New nomination recorded

### Address Change
- Submit address proof
- Update in system
- KYC required

## Important Information for Branch Staff

### Customer Education
- Explain FD features clearly
- Show interest and maturity calculations
- Explain TDS and Form 15G/15H
- Explain premature withdrawal penalty
- Suggest FD laddering for better returns

### Compliance
- Verify PAN for cash deposits of ₹50,000+
- Obtain Form 60/61 if PAN not available
- Ensure nomination is completed
- Check money laundering indicators

### Do's
- Explain the interest rate and maturity amount
- Explain TDS deduction
- Explain premature withdrawal penalty
- Suggest FD laddering
- Provide copy of FD terms and conditions

### Don'ts
- Don't mislead about interest rates
- Don't promise returns higher than published rates
- Don't accept cash beyond ₹50,000 without PAN
- Don't forget TDS if applicable
- Don't ignore nomination

## FD Interest Certificate

For Income Tax Filing:
- Issue interest certificate for financial year
- Shows all FDs and interest earned
- Shows TDS deducted
- Required for ITR filing
- Can be generated from system or obtained from branch

## Special Cases

### Minor FD
- Opened by guardian
- Guardian operates till minor becomes major
- On attaining majority: Convert to regular FD
- Date of birth proof required

### Joint FD
- Can hold jointly with family members
- "Former or Survivor" or "Either or Survivor"
- First holder is primary owner
- Tax implication: First holder pays tax

### NRI FD (NRE/NRO)
- NRE FD: Tax-free interest, repatriable
- NRO FD: Interest taxable, non-repatriable
- NRI can't hold normal resident FD
- NRE/NRO funds should be from NRE/NRO account
