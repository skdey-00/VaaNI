# Account Opening Process

This document outlines the RBI-compliant process for opening various types of bank accounts in India.

## Types of Accounts

1. **Savings Account** - For individuals to deposit savings and earn interest
2. **Current Account** - For businesses, LLPs, companies with unlimited transactions
3. **Salary Account** - For salary credit with zero balance requirement
4. **Senior Citizen Savings Account** - For citizens aged 60+ with higher interest rates
5. **Joint Account** - Two or more individuals as joint holders

## Account Opening Procedure

### Step 1: Customer Approach and Initial Discussion
- Greet the customer professionally
- Understand customer requirements and account type preference
- Explain features of different account types
- Provide information on interest rates, minimum balance requirements, and charges
- For business accounts: verify business entity type and registration status

### Step 2: Document Collection
Collect the following mandatory documents:

**For Individuals (KYC Documents):**
- PAN Card (mandatory for all account types)
- Aadhaar Card (preferred for e-KYC)
- Recent passport-size photograph (2 copies)
- Proof of Address (if Aadhaar address is not current):
  - Passport
  - Voter ID Card
  - Driving License
  - Utility bill (electricity/telephone/water) not older than 2 months
  - Bank statement with address (not older than 3 months)

**For NRIs (NRE/NRO Accounts):**
- Valid Passport with visa stamp
- Overseas address proof
- PAN Card (or Form 60 if not available)
- Recent passport-size photograph

**For Companies/LLPs/Partnerships:**
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration
- Memorandum & Articles of Association
- Board Resolution for account opening
- List of Directors/Partners with photographs
- PAN of the entity
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)

### Step 3: Know Your Customer (KYC) Verification
As per RBI KYC norms, follow one of these methods:

**a) Aadhaar-based e-KYC (Preferred):**
1. Ask customer for Aadhaar number
2. Initiate OTP-based authentication
3. Verify OTP received on customer's registered mobile
4. For biometric e-KYC: Use fingerprint/iris scanner device
5. Retrieve customer details from UIDAI database
6. Complete CKYCR (Central KYC Records Registry) verification

**b) Offline Aadhaar KYC:**
1. Download Aadhaar XML using customer's consent
2. Securely share password with customer
3. Verify Aadhaar details
4. Ensure Aadhaar is masked (first 8 digits hidden)

**c) Paper-based KYC (Offline Mode):**
1. Physically verify original documents
2. Take self-attested copies
3. Mention "Original verified and returned" on copies
4. Sign and stamp as verifier

### Step 4: Filling Account Opening Form (AOF)
Complete the Account Opening Form with:
- Customer's full name (as per PAN)
- Father's/Spouse's name
- Date of Birth
- Gender
- Marital status
- Permanent address and communication address
- Mobile number and email ID
- PAN number
- Occupation details
- Annual income range
- Nomination details (nominee name, relationship, address)

Important notes:
- Ensure all mandatory fields are filled
- Nomination is mandatory as per RBI guidelines
- For minor accounts: provide guardian details and minor's DOB proof
- For joint accounts: get details of all holders and mode of operation

### Step 5: Form 60/61 Declaration (if PAN not available)
If customer does not have PAN:
- Fill Form 60 (for individuals) or Form 61 (for entities)
- Declare reasons for not having PAN
- Get customer signature/thumb impression
- Note: PAN is mandatory for most accounts; Form 60 only in specific cases

### Step 6: Compliance Checks
Before proceeding, verify:
- Customer is not in any sanction list (UN/OFAC)
- No existing alerts in CKYCR
- For high-value accounts: Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) required
- For politically exposed persons (PEPs): Branch manager approval required

### Step 7: Initial Deposit
Receive initial deposit amount:
- Accept cash, cheque, or transfer from existing account
- Minimum balance requirements:
  - Savings Account: ₹1,000 - ₹10,000 (varies by bank)
  - Current Account: ₹10,000 - ₹25,000
  - Premium Savings: ₹50,000+
  - Salary Account: Zero balance

For cheque deposits:
- Note cheque number, date, and drawee bank
- Write "For opening account with [customer name]" on cheque
- Clearing period applies (funds available after clearance)

### Step 8: Account Number Generation and Welcome Kit
- Generate unique customer ID and account number
- Allocate CIF (Customer Information File) number
- Provide welcome kit containing:
  - Passbook
  - Debit Card (PIN will be sent separately)
  - Cheque book (if requested)
  - Internet banking credentials
  - Mobile banking activation details
  - Account opening welcome letter

### Step 9: Facility Activation
Activate the following facilities:
1. **Debit Card**: Link to account, set PIN
2. **Internet Banking**: Share customer ID and temporary password
3. **Mobile Banking**: Guide for app download and registration
4. **SMS Alerts**: Register mobile number for transaction alerts
5. **Standing Instructions**: If any auto-debits requested

### Step 10: Customer Orientation
Brief the customer about:
- How to operate the account
- Use of ATM/debit card
- Internet and mobile banking features
- Balance maintenance requirements
- Charges for non-maintenance of minimum balance
- Interest crediting cycle (quarterly for savings)
- Customer care contact details

## Important Compliance Notes

1. **PAN Exemption**: Only allowed for Small Accounts (Basic Savings Bank Deposit Account - BSBDA) with deposits up to ₹50,000 and credits up to ₹1 lakh per year
2. **Small Account**: Can be opened with self-declaration and simplified KYC, valid for 12 months initially
3. **Nomination**: Mandatory for all deposit accounts as per Banking Regulation Act
4. **Freezing of Accounts**: If KYC not updated within reasonable time, account operations can be restricted
5. **Minor Accounts**: For children below 10 years, account operated by guardian; for 10-18 years, can be operated jointly

| PAN Card | Mandatory | Mandatory | Mandatory | Mandatory |

## Common Scenarios and Handling

1. **Customer has only Aadhaar, no PAN:**
   - Open BSBDA (Small Account)
   - Explain limitations: ₹50,000 max balance, ₹1 lakh max credit
   - Advise to apply for PAN

2. **Address on Aadhaar is old:**
   - Accept Aadhaar as identity proof
   - Get separate address proof (utility bill, passport, etc.)

3. **Customer is illiterate:**
   - Allow thumb impression instead of signature
   - Use left thumb impression (LTI)
   - Get thumb attested by an independent witness
   - Take photograph of customer giving thumb impression

4. **Minor wants to open account:**
   - Below 10 years: Minor account operated by guardian
   - 10-18 years: Can operate independently
   - Minor turns major: Convert to regular account

## Turnaround Time (TAT)

- **For normal account opening**: 1-2 working days
- **For e-KYC with instant account**: Same day (within 2 hours)
- **For companies/LLPs**: 3-5 working days (requires additional verification)
